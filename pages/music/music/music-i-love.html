julie live



this entry basically counts for any julie performance ever. this just happens to be a video i really fucking love. i love their music but man i also love the way they perform. they don't say anything, don't make faces. the music speaks for itself. i hate when artists are like 'open up the pit!!! open up the pitt!!! HELLO TORONTO!!! moooshpit moooshpit mooooshpit'. these guys just get on stage and perform and get the fuck off. its the coolest shit ever. no emotion. the way keyan pourzand (the guitarist in the middle) moves his body and bends his knees while playing the guitar is so sick. he looks so in control. all of them do. i feel like, when you dont force a certain energy (by, for example, screaming really loud in songs, or banging your head really hard, or encouraging the crowd to do anything, like open a moshpit or something) you let the audience experience your music for themselves. the artist isnt ASKING you to feel any kind of way. its almost as if the music just exists on its own and the beauty within it is found rather than given. its beautiful in the same way trees are. or flowers. or clouds. or bugs. exact and calculated, detailed and intricate, yet completely organic, simply existing, not asking you to interact with it and not reacting if you do. thats how i feel when i watch or go to julie shows. and usually i get really frustrated when im the only one seeing beauty in something. but i know that behind their cold faces, they also understand what i see in them. im getting really deep for no reason, i just really really love this band. i honest to god think theyre THE greatest young rock band operating today. shoutout to alex brady who plays bass and sings, keyan pourzand who plays guitar and sings, and dillon lee who is a fucking beast at drumming i love him so much. he also drew all the cover arts for julie hes a talented artist as well. and lastly i wanna say through your window and lochness are some of julie's best and most iconic songs imo, both of which happen to be in this video... watch it all the way through

ronnie red - brownie

Ronnie Red · Brownie


beautiful song where the lyrics are what makes it good. something really rare for me since im usually a producerhead and i listen to the production and chords and sound design and textures and mixing and stuff (i dont even know the lyrics or meaning to 9 out of my 10 fav songs). this song is basically about a dog named brownie that went blind, and the young child of who's perspective its written from empathizing with her. the line 'you said she'd be fine, but i saw her cry, when she bumped her head against the wall, but what could you do for an old dog like you' is so so so so moving. absolute masterpiece of songwriting that, in my eyes, perfected the concept of 'show, don't tell' in songwriting. which is basically just that if your story is moving enough you won't have to describe or show how you felt during it, you just have to say what happened and that'll be moving on its own. much more so actually because the listeners imagines it playing out in their head. they just experience it and feel what they would feel in that situation. in this song im like oh my god poor fucking thing. ronnie red doesnt go 'im so sad that my dog went blind.. oh no' he just tells the story and whatever im just repeating myself. theres a lot of songs especially in like emo rap where the lyrics literally say 'i wanna kill myself and i feel depressed' which yeah if youre a teenager going thru the same thing, hearing someone describe those feelings, you'll relate to it and be like yeah i get what hes saying. but it wont move you like this kind of storytelling will. beautiful song

quadeca - forgone



a real tear-inducer for me. its an 8 minute song but totally earns its length. the song starts out with this extremely textured morphing blend of sounds that transforms and changes shape until going quiet, and the song 'starts', with this fucking GORGEOUS chord progression with accompanying vocal harmonies that sound like they're sung by 1500-year old dead spirits. his vocals are mixed in a weird non-conventional way that makes it feel like they were possessed and taken over by the instrumental. they sit in between the instruments instead on top of them, leaving a lot of room for the insanely lush and overpowering production. perfect example of learning the rules of mixing then breaking them with taste to drive the artistic point of the song home. i mean when it comes to achieving a feeling through purely sound this song fucking nails it...
